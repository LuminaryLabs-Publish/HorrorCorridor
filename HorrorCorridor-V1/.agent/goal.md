# Goal

Status: active

## Objective

Build the complete Horror Corridor Expedition domain-service composition against the current NexusEngine core-kit architecture, turn the canonical target tree into installed runtime behavior, and keep iterating until the whole expedition plays smoothly and the corridor environment visibly matches the generated room target.

## Success Criteria

- Reconcile every domain, state group, kit, service, host, and proof in `.agent/horror-corridor-domain-tree.md` with an executable installed composition rather than leaving the tree as documentation only.
- Reuse current NexusEngine core kits and promoted domain services wherever their contracts fit; keep only HorrorCorridor-specific composition, presets, content, and genuinely missing reusable behavior local.
- Give stateful local domains explicit ownership, inputs, outputs, dependencies, idempotent installation, reset behavior, snapshot behavior, and runtime proof.
- Keep gameplay rules out of React, Three.js, PeerJS, and browser adapters; those hosts consume domain state and descriptors through controlled boundaries.
- Use `docs/visual-targets/horror-corridor-expedition-room-v2.png` as the active room target, then bring the live corridor toward its tight ruined-service-chamber composition, material identity, wet response, localized light, readable route, and threatening far threshold.
- Prove the full expedition flow: start, load, cross the intro, traverse successive buildings, hear and answer monsters, repel early, survive and collect a full scare, claim or pass a room offer, continue, lose by capture, restart, reconnect, and recover without breaking authoritative state.
- Prove smooth play through frame pacing, continuous movement/look, bounded network/UI cadence, stable collision, clean default play view, and spawn/movement/look screenshots.
- Keep build, lint, typecheck, core composition, smoke, reset/snapshot/replay, package/export, live-player, and human-view gates passing at the scope each gate actually covers.
